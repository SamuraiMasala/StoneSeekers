The Museum

We chose the Luigi Bombicci Museum, is part of the University of Bologna's museum network (SMA), which includes 15 museums showcasing diverse cultural and scientific heritage since 1088.

What you can see:

  • Around 9,000 mineral specimens, including over 3,000 systematically classified by chemical and structural types.
  • Unique collections such as:
    • Rare meteorites
    • Over 400 Sicilian amber pieces
    • Precious stones including gold nuggets and diamonds
    • Historical scientific instruments like microscopes, protractors, and spectroscopes.

  • A special fluorescent minerals booth, where visitors can enjoy glowing minerals in a dark environment.

The Mineralogical Collection "Luigi Bombicci Museum"


About the founder Luigi Bombicci:

  • Founder of the museum and a leading mineralogist at the University of Bologna.
  • Appointed professor at age 27, focused on Italian minerals and related sciences.
  • Passionate educator and researcher who contributed significantly to mineralogy and science education reform.

Location and Exhibition Layout

The Luigi Bombicci Mineralogical Museum is located in the historic center of Bologna, at Piazza di Porta San Donato. It occupies the first floor of the University of Bologna’s Department of Earth Sciences building.

  • Exhibition space: 850 m²

  • The layout is divided into several main areas (see the map/image):
    Orange area (#1–10): Main systematic mineralogical collection featuring minerals from around the world.
    Green area (#20–29): Minerals sourced from various regions of Italy.
    Pink area (#18–19): Minerals specifically from Bologna.
    Top area (#13–17): Petrological samples (rock specimens).
    Additional smaller display cases scattered throughout show a variety of minerals of different types and sizes

Institutional Goals

After conducting an on-site visit and observing the museum environment, we identified three institutional goals for our project:

  • Use existing assets in new ways: We want to make the most of the rich, high quality collection it by creating a meaningful visitor experience. 
  • Change visitor attitudes: Mineralogy may seem complex or uninviting—but it doesn’t have to be. We want to show how interesting this world can be, even for those with no background in the field.
  • Increase visitor numbers and outreach: Despite its value, the museum remains little known. Our project aims to boost its visibility and welcome a wider audience.
